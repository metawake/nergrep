Southern Rock emerged in the 1970s from the American South. The Allman Brothers Band, formed in Jacksonville, Florida, pioneered this genre, while Lynyrd Skynyrd, also from Jacksonville, popularized it worldwide with hits like "Sweet Home Alabama". Other notable bands include The Marshall Tucker Band from South Carolina.

Python, created by Guido van Rossum in 1991 at CWI in the Netherlands, has become one of the world's most popular programming languages. Companies like Google, Instagram, and Spotify heavily rely on Python for their operations. The Python Software Foundation, established in 2001, manages the language's development.

Greece, officially the Hellenic Republic, is a country in southeastern Europe. Athens, its capital and largest city, is considered the birthplace of democracy. The Parthenon, built in the 5th century BC on the Acropolis, remains one of the world's most famous architectural monuments. Alexander the Great, born in Pella, spread Greek culture across his vast empire.

Sergio Leone, an Italian filmmaker, revolutionized the Western genre with his "Dollars Trilogy" starring Clint Eastwood. His masterpiece "Once Upon a Time in America" (1984), featuring Robert De Niro, was produced by Warner Bros. Leone's distinctive style influenced directors like Quentin Tarantino.

The British Mail system, now known as Royal Mail, was established in 1516 during the reign of Henry VIII. The Penny Black, introduced in 1840, was the world's first adhesive postage stamp. Sir Rowland Hill's postal reforms transformed communication across the British Empire.

Aspirin, first synthesized by Felix Hoffmann at Bayer in 1897, became one of the most widely used medications globally. The U.S. Food and Drug Administration approved its use in 1899. The World Health Organization includes aspirin on its List of Essential Medicines.

Microsoft Corporation, founded by Bill Gates and Paul Allen in 1975 in Albuquerque, New Mexico, became the world's largest software company. Windows, released in 1985, revolutionized personal computing. The company's headquarters in Redmond, Washington, spans a massive campus.

The International Space Station (ISS), a joint project between NASA, ESA, JAXA, and the Canadian Space Agency, has been continuously occupied since 2000. SpaceX, founded by Elon Musk, now regularly delivers supplies to the ISS from Kennedy Space Center.

The Beatles, formed in Liverpool in 1960, became the most influential band in popular music history. John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, and Ringo Starr worked with producer George Martin at Abbey Road Studios to create groundbreaking albums like "Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band".

Mount Everest, standing at 29,029 feet (8,848 meters), was first successfully climbed by Sir Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay in 1953. The Nepalese government and the Chinese Tibet Autonomous Region share the mountain's administration. The Sherpa people have played a crucial role in Himalayan mountaineering.
